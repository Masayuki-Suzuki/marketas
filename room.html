Rooms & Rates

At Marketa’s Bed and Breakfast you will find Edwardian comfort at every turn. The warmth of wood, from paneled walls to four poster beds (ornately decorated by Marketa herself) infuses this bed and breakfast treat with its calming influence. Each room is individually decorated and has special features and personal touches (including Marketa’s own artwork).Whether you are visiting us on a family break, a romantic getaway, or business, our uniquely decorated rooms will enchant you. We have rooms to fit most budgets and can accommodate various size groups of all age’s even our younger guests are catered to with baby cribs and rollaway beds.For that special weekend Marketa’s Bed and Breakfast invites you to try one of our feature rooms. Experience the luxury of a beautiful four-poster bed, fireplace and jetted tub in our Edwardian heritage house.

Choose from private or shared baths and queen or four-poster beds. Our B&B accommodations offer the personal service and ambience of a bed and breakfast that our guests expect with an inn’s charm.

history

Marketa opened her B&B Victoria BC in 1996. With the help of her young son Jacob and Mr. T the lovable family dog (a real character!) she runs a warm and friendly hostelry accommodation with European flair, close to downtown Victoria. Her love of cooking is obvious to anyone who has sampled her gourmet breakfasts, which are a favourite of guests.

This beautiful Edwardian mansion was built in 1912. The first owner of the house was a widow with four children who bought it because she wanted something smaller. She was obviously wealthy because, even by today’s standards, the house is large.

reviews

As written up in Country Pleasures Magazine - By Mary Mendoza

The odds of me ever getting to Prague are about the same as Martha Stewart swimming the English Channel clad only in a KMart towel. Happily I can experience some of the flavor of that beautiful Czech city with a visit to Marketa’s Bed and Breakfast in Victoria.

Marketa’s possesses the qualities one might find in a small Eastern European inn–at bargain prices. Its complete lack of pretentiousness and fun-filled atmosphere put it on the top of my list of favorite places to stay in Victoria.

Then there is the woman behind it all, innkeeper Marketa Clark. A stunning Czech native, married to a Canadian computer-guru and mother to a young son, Marketa devotes her spare time to her great passion – painting. She is an accomplished artist whose works have appeared in galleries in Europe and British Columbia. A number of her original oil paintings are displayed at the B&B.
